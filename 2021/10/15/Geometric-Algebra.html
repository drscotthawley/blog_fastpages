Kinds of products, AKA WTH is a Kronecker product?

The paper uses "Kronecker Product". Which...I didn't know what that is. I see on the internet that it's likened to the "tensor product". Which is like the "outer product". But is different from "exterior product" (even though "outer" and "exterior" should mean the same thing, they don't) because math history. But the latter is the same as the wedge product. ...Yes?

...And all these are of course different from the dot product which is the same as "matrix multiplication" which is the same as the "@" symbol.
